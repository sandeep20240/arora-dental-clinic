OPG (Orthopantomogram)

An orthopantomogram (OPG) is a type of X-ray that provides a panoramic view of the lower face, showing all teeth, including wisdom teeth, and details of the jawbone and temporomandibular joint (TMJ). The procedure involves the patient positioning their chin on a shelf and biting on a mouthpiece while the X-ray machine rotates around their head. It's quick, painless, and allows patients to resume their normal activities immediately afterward.

OPG X-rays Overview:

  • Special dental imaging shows a complete view of your mouth in one picture.
  • Includes upper and lower jaws, teeth, surrounding bones, and jaw joints (TMJ).

Uses of OPG X-rays:

  • Helpful for diagnosing dental issues like impacted teeth, jaw problems, and bone irregularities.
  • Dentists rely on OPG X-rays to plan orthodontic treatments, wisdom teeth removal, and dental implants.

Advantages of OPG X-rays:

  • More comfortable and less invasive compared to traditional intraoral X-rays.
  • Low radiation exposure, making them a safer choice for regular dental checkups.

Procedure of OPG X-ray

  • Preparation: When you arrive for the OPG X-ray, you'll be asked to remove any jewelry, eyeglasses, or metallic objects that could interfere with the imaging process.
  • Positioning: The dental professional will position you correctly in front of the OPG X-ray machine, ensuring you are standing or sitting upright and aligned properly.
  • Bite Wing: You'll be asked to bite down gently on a bite wing or a plastic support to keep your teeth and jaws in the correct position.
  • Staying Still: It's essential to remain as still as possible during the procedure to obtain clear and accurate images.
  • Machine Alignment: The OPG X-ray machine will rotate around your head in a circular motion while capturing images. You won't feel any discomfort during this process.
  • Minimal Radiation: OPG X-rays emit a minimal amount of radiation compared to other dental X-rays, ensuring safety for patients.
  • Image Capture: As the machine rotates, it captures multiple images to create a panoramic view of your oral cavity, including your upper and lower jaws, teeth, and surrounding structures.
